"AlphaFold3 predictions reveal that LUCA ProRS and ThrRS both exhibit equal
binding affinity for proline and threonine (pocket ipTM = 0.78 and 0.70,

catalytic efficiency without narrowing substrate specificity."

RESULTS - Discovery #2 (Receiver-First):
------------------------------------------
"AlphaFold3 predictions reveal a striking asymmetry in LUCA ProRS confidence
scores: pocket ipTM (0.78) substantially exceeds global ipTM (0.28),
indicating the substrate binding site evolved high specificity before the
global protein structure achieved full coordination (Figure 5A). In contrast,
modern E. coli ProRS shows equivalent pocket and global ipTM scores (0.95
for both; Figure 5B), demonstrating subsequent evolutionary optimization of
overall structural rigidity."

DISCUSSION - Combined Interpretation:
---------------------------------------
"The maintenance of substrate promiscuity from LUCA to modern bacteria
(promiscuity index = 1.00 for all enzymes; Table 1) indicates that substrate
selectivity in contemporary aaRS enzymes arises primarily through post-binding
mechanisms (catalysis rates, editing domain activity) rather than differential
substrate recognition. The receiver-first pattern (Figure 5) reveals that this
promiscuous binding pocket evolved under strong selection before the global
protein structure optimized, suggesting a modular evolutionary strategy
prioritizing functional competence over structural elegance."
